Abstract

Drone monitoring enables rapid observation of wide areas without requiring dedicated infrastructure, so it can be utilized in various fields such as disaster management, environmental surveillance, and agricultural monitoring. However, with advances in camera technology and increased drone flight times, the collection of massive high-resolution images leads to significant transmission delays and increased computational complexity in generating integrated views. To address these challenges, this thesis proposes (i) a transmission protocol optimized for local network environments and (ii) an image selection technique leveraging pre-computed feature point matching. The proposed transmission protocol maximizes throughput while ensuring minimal reliability in local network environments with low congestion and packet loss, achieving a 59\% improvement in transmission speed compared to conventional TCP while guaranteeing complete data transfer. The image selection technique reduces transmission and storage requirements by up to 90\% while minimizing degradation in integrated view quality, and decreases the time required for integrated view generation. Both techniques have been integrated into the open-source project OpenDroneMap (ODM), demonstrating their applicability in real-world drone monitoring pipelines.
